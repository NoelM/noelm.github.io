Build a mag loop antenna

The magnetic loop antennas have a lot of advantages: they are compact and are pretty efficient. So, they are quite relevant when the QRA is in a flat, and also for indoor use. Actually, that's my case having the QRA located in a parisian flat. Deploying a full antenna l/4 antenna for HF is complex even with a balcony, because the antenna would be in front of my neighbors window.

Mag loop basics

The small antennas consist of two loop, an inner one and a outer one. The inner loop is directly connected to the coax, an end to the shield, the other one to the core. The inner loop acts like a Faraday loop with the second one. The outer loop is not connected to the inner one. Thus the outer loop is inductively fed by the inner one.

However, the outer loop must be tuned correctly with the help of a capacitor. Because the bandwidth of the antenna is small, the capacitor has to be variable even on the same band!

Finding the good parameters

I advise you to use the excellent tool VK3CPU Magloop Antenna Calculator. It computes for you all the equation and also gives you the perfect parameters for: efficiency, capacitance, voltage, Q value.

How to proceed?

First of all, you need to find the parameters ⌀a and ⌀b, respectively the diameters of the inner and outer loop. My advise would to initially toggle only the efficiency (ratio of energy transmitted to RF with respect to heat) and the Q-value. So, you can find the best enough efficiency for your antenna at the desired band, usually, they are pretty compact up to 20m. If you search for a 40m or even a 80m mag loop antenna, the only solution will be to add more coils, you can vary it with N.
